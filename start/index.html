Potrait of Elanor of Toledo

The potrait of Elanor of Toledo and Her Son is a painting by the Italian artist Agnolo di Cosimo, known as Bronzino, finshed ca. 1545. One of his most famous works, it is housed in the Uffizi Gallery of Florence, Italy and is considered one of the preeminent examples of Mannerist portraiture.

Potrait of the Artist

Potrait of the Artist Holding a Thistle is an oil painting on parchment pasted on canvas by a Geramn artist Albrecht Durer, painted in 1493. It is the earliest of Durer's painted self-portraits and has been identified as one of the first self-portraits painted by Northern artist.

Gril with a Pearl Earring

Girl with a Pearl Earring is one of Johannes Vermeer's masterworks and, as the name implies, uses a pearl earring for a focal point. Today the painting is kept in the Mauritshuis gallery in The Hague. It is sometimes referred to as "the Mona Lisa of the North" or "the Dutch Mona Lisa".
